# 🧹 GitHub Repository Cleanup Guide

To make your "Repositories" tab look professional for Data Analytics recruiters, follow these steps to add descriptions and hide unrelated projects.

---

### 1. Add Descriptions & Topics (Metadata) 🏷️
Recruiters search for keywords. Update your key repositories with these specific details:

#### **[Data-Analytics-Portfolio](https://github.com/iamharsh00/Data-Analytics-Portfolio)**
- **Description:** `End-to-end data analytics projects including E-commerce Churn Analysis, Food Recommenders, and Algorithmic Trading Backtesting (Python, Pandas, ML).`
- **Topics (Tags):** `data-analytics`, `python`, `machine-learning`, `exploratory-data-analysis`, `pandas`, `visualization`, `sql`

#### **[MIND-BEHIND-CODE-PORTFOLIO](https://github.com/iamharsh00/MIND-BEHIND-CODE-PORTFOLIO)**
- **Description:** `Personal portfolio website built with HTML/CSS/JS to showcase data analytics expertise and project visualizations.`
- **Topics (Tags):** `portfolio`, `data-analytics`, `frontend`, `visualization`

---

### 2. Clean Up Clutter (Java/JS Learning Tasks) 🗑️
Repositories like `100-days-javascript`, `DOM-Project-In-JS`, and `DRY-CODE-TASK-IN-JAVA` make your profile look like a beginner. 

**Recommendation:**
1. Go to the **Settings** tab of these repositories.
2. Scroll to the bottom ("Danger Zone").
3. Click **"Change visibility"** and set them to **Private**.
4. *Alternatively*, click **"Archive this repository"** to show it is no longer active.

---

### 3. Standardization Check ✅
Make sure all your high-priority repositories follow a similar style. Using descriptions and topics on your top 2-3 repositories will immediately make the page look more "filled out" and professional.

---
**Recruiters want to see focus.** By highlighting your Data Analytics work and moving older learning tasks to private, you show that you are specialized and ready for a professional role.
